What is a comedy club night?

Live stand-up in a small room. A compere warms up the crowd and links the evening, then several acts perform, usually building towards a headliner. London's comedy circuit is one of the best in the world, and on any given night you might catch someone who'll be on television in two years' time.

What to expect

  • A compere who works the room and introduces each act.
  • Three to five acts across the night, of varying experience.
  • An interval — a chance to get to the bar.
  • A headliner to close, usually the most established name.
  • Two to three hours in total.

The front row question

Honest warning: if you sit at the front, you will be spoken to. Comperes almost always chat to the first couple of rows — asking names, jobs, who you're with. It's rarely cruel, but if the thought makes you uncomfortable, sit further back. You'll see just as well.

And a related rule: don't heckle unless you genuinely want a professional comedian's full attention. They've heard it before, and they will win.

Brilliant solo

A comedy night is one of the easiest things to do alone in London. Sit at the back or at the bar, and nobody notices or cares. You're facing forward watching a show — it doesn't require a companion. Cheap, too, at around £22.

Practical notes

  • Book ahead for the better-known clubs, especially at weekends.
  • Most clubs are 18+.
  • Arrive early for a decent seat, particularly at unreserved venues.
  • Material can be near the knuckle — it's stand-up, not a family show.

Frequently asked questions

What happens at a comedy club night?

A compere warms up the room and introduces several acts across the evening, usually building to a headliner. Nights typically run two to three hours with an interval, and most clubs have a bar.

Will you get picked on at a comedy club?

Only really if you sit in the front row. Comperes commonly chat to the front few tables, so sit further back if you'd rather not be part of the show. Heckling invites a response, so don't unless you want one.

Can you go to a comedy club alone?

Yes, and plenty do. Sit at the back or the bar and nobody will pay you the slightest attention. It's one of the easier nights out to enjoy on your own.
