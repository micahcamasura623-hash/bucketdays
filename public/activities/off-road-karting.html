What is off-road karting?

Off-road karting takes the go-kart you know and drops it onto dirt. Instead of a smooth indoor circuit, you're sliding around a muddy outdoor track with bumps, ruts and corners that genuinely test you. It's messier, more chaotic and — for many people — considerably more fun than indoor karting.

Why it's the best value adrenaline activity

At around £30, off-road karting is the cheapest genuine adrenaline hit near London. Some venues even offer full-day track access at that price, which makes it exceptional value compared to a £70 buggy session or a £230 skydive. If you want maximum thrill per pound, this is it.

What to expect on the day

  • Safety briefing — the track rules, flags and how the kart works.
  • Kitting up — helmet provided; some venues offer overalls.
  • Driving — you drive solo, at your own pace, with marshals watching.
  • Sessions or full days — depending on the venue, you'll get timed sessions or open track time.

Karting vs rage buggy: which should you pick?

Both are off-road, both need no licence. The honest difference:

  • Karting (£30) — cheaper, lower to the ground, more about racing lines and speed.
  • Rage buggy (£70) — more substantial vehicle, roll cage and harness, more of an "event" feel.

If budget matters, start with karting. If you want the bigger experience, go buggy.

What to wear

  • Clothes you're happy to ruin — you will get muddy.
  • Trainers or boots, never open shoes.
  • A change of clothes for the journey home.
  • Helmets are provided.

Frequently asked questions

Do you need a licence for off-road karting?

No. Off-road karting takes place on private tracks rather than public roads, so no driving licence is needed. Venues set their own minimum age, so check when booking.

How much does off-road karting cost?

Off-road karting is one of the cheapest adrenaline activities near London, typically starting from around £30 for a session, with some venues offering full-day access at that price.

What should you wear for off-road karting?

Wear clothes you don't mind getting muddy and covered in dirt, along with trainers or sturdy closed-toe shoes. Helmets and safety gear are provided by the venue.
