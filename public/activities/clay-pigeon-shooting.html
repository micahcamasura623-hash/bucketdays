What is clay pigeon shooting?

Clay pigeon shooting involves firing a shotgun at clay discs launched into the air, simulating the flight of a bird. It's a sport of timing and instinct rather than brute skill — which is exactly why it works so well as a beginner experience. A qualified instructor sets everything up, coaches you through it, and most people are hitting clays within the first twenty minutes.

What to expect on the day

  • Safety briefing — gun handling, range rules, and what to expect.
  • One-to-one coaching — your instructor guides your stance, grip, aim and timing.
  • Shooting — you'll work through a set number of clays (a typical beginner package is 25).
  • Feedback — the instructor helps you correct and improve with each shot.

A session typically runs one to two hours, including the briefing.

Do you need a licence or experience?

Neither. At a registered shooting ground you shoot under the direct supervision of a qualified instructor, which means you don't need to hold a shotgun licence yourself — the ground's certificate covers you. And no prior experience is expected; beginner sessions are specifically designed for people who've never held a shotgun before.

What's included

  • Shotgun and cartridges
  • Clays (usually 25 for a beginner package)
  • Ear and eye protection
  • Full instruction from a certified coach

You genuinely just need to turn up — everything else is provided.

What to wear

  • Comfortable outdoor clothing suitable for the weather.
  • Sturdy shoes or boots — grounds can be muddy.
  • Layers, as you'll be standing outdoors for a while.
  • Avoid loose scarves or anything that could catch.

Who is it good for?

Clay pigeon shooting suits almost anyone — it doesn't require strength or fitness, just focus. It's a popular birthday activity, works brilliantly solo or in a group, and gives a real sense of achievement the first time you powder a clay out of the sky.

Frequently asked questions

Do you need a licence for clay pigeon shooting?

No. At a registered shooting ground you can shoot under the supervision of a qualified instructor without holding a shotgun licence, as the ground's own certificate covers you.

Is clay pigeon shooting hard for beginners?

Not at all. Instructors set everything up and coach you through stance, grip and aim. Most complete beginners hit their first clay within the first twenty minutes.

What should you wear for clay pigeon shooting?

Wear comfortable outdoor clothing suitable for the weather, and sturdy shoes or boots as grounds can be muddy. Ear and eye protection are provided.
